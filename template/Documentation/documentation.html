How to change template variables

It's recommended to use Sass to generate your own color skin.

Sass

We've prepared 3 sets of variables: Basketball, Soccer and American Football versions. You can find them here:

  • source/sass/config/_variables-basketball.scss
  • source/sass/config/_variables-soccer.scss

Open source/sass/config/_imports.scss and set which version you want to use. Please check next variations:

'Basketball' 
@import "variables-basketball";
@import "mixins";
'Basketball - Dark' 
@import "variables-basketball";
@import "variables-basketball-dark";
@import "mixins";
'Soccer' 
@import "variables-soccer";
@import "mixins";
'Soccer - Dark' 
@import "variables-soccer";
@import "variables-soccer-dark";
@import "mixins";
'American Football' 
@import "variables-football";
@import "mixins";

After that you can change any variables, including color variables:

  1. Open source/sass/config/_variables-basketball.scss in your favorite code editor.
  2. Change the value of color variable: $color-primary: #ffdc11 !default; with your own.
  3. Compile your Sass to CSS.

Instagram Widget

Instagram widget allows you to add your Instagram photos to your website.

To fetch images specifically from your account, set the get and userId options:

var insta_feed = new Instafeed({
  get: 'user',
  target: 'instagram-feed',
  userId: 'YOUR_USER_ID',
  accessToken: 'YOUR_ACCESS_TOKEN',
  limit: 6,
  template: '
    • '
});
    Note: YOUR_USER_ID option corresponds to your Instagram account ID (eg: 4385108), not your username. If you do not know your account ID, do a quick google search for "What is my Instagram account ID?". There a several free tools available online that will look it up for you, for example this one.
    Note: YOUR_ACCESS_TOKEN (Instagram Access Token) is a long string of characters unique to your account that grants other applications access to your Instagram feed. In order to display your Instagram photos on your own website, you are required to provide an Instagram Access Token. There a several free tools available online that will look it up for you, for example this one.

    Social Counters Widget

    Social Counters widget display number of your social media fans.

    The Twitter counter require PHP to work, all other counters only require jQuery.

    To fetch your social media fans, followers etc. you need to setup the widget. Open assets/js/init.js and Social Counter init.

    // Facebook
facebook_user: 'FACEBOOK_USER_ID',
facebook_token: 'FACEBOOK_TOKEN',

// Google+
google_plus_id: 'GOOGLE_PLUS_ID',
google_plus_key: 'GOOGLE_PLUS_KEY',

// Instagram
instagram_user: 'INSTAGRAM_USER_ID',
instagram_token: 'INSTAGRAM_TOKEN',

// Twitter
twitter_user: 'danfisher_dev',

    Facebook. Please read next article how to setup Facebook counter https://smashballoon.com/custom-facebook-feed/access-token/

    Google+. Please read next article how to setup Google+ counter https://developers.google.com/identity/protocols/OAuth2
